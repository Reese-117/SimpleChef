# Instructions for Final Project Report:
1. Download the HCI_Final_Project_Report__Template_.zip file under Files in Canvas.
2. Go to Overleaf.com and create a new project by selecting "Upload Project".
3. Upload the .zip file directly — Overleaf will automatically extract and set up all the files for you.

*Note: Use only the provided IEEE 2-column format for the report.*
The following should be the main contents of your report:
1. Abstract
2. Introduction
3. Implementation
4. Methodology
5. Testing
6. Results and Discussion
7. Conclusion
8. Appendix

The provided template is designed to help you structure your report in the IEEE two-column format. Follow these instructions to customize the template for your specific project.
## 1. Understanding the Template Structure

The template is organized into several .tex files, each corresponding to a section of your report. The main file, main.tex, compiles these sections into a cohesive document.

## 2. Editing the Title and Author Information

- Open main.tex.
- Update the \title{} and \author{} fields with your project title and your name, respectively.
- Ensure your email and department information are accurate.

## 3. Customizing the Abstract

- Open abstract.tex.
- Replace the placeholder text with a concise summary of your project, highlighting the objectives, methods, and key findings.

## 4. Completing the Introduction

- Open introduction.tex.
- Provide background information on your project, including the motivation and significance of your work.
- Clearly define the scope of your project and outline the objectives.

## 5. Detailing the Implementation

- Open implementation.tex.
- Describe the tools and technologies you used in your project.
- Discuss the development environment and any specific configurations or setups you employed.

## 6. Explaining the Methodology

- Open methodology.tex.
- Describe the system architecture and provide a detailed block diagram explaining how the system works step-by-step.
- Include a hierarchical task analysis or entity-relationship model for your project. 
- Add a Gantt chart to show the project timeline and milestones.

## 7. Documenting Testing Procedures

- Open testing.tex.
- Detail the user testing and usability testing procedures you conducted.
- Include any feedback you received and how it influenced your project.

## 8. Presenting Results and Discussion

- Open results_discussion.tex.
- Summarize the results of your testing and analysis.
- Discuss the implications of your findings and how they relate to your project objectives.
- Include a performance analysis of your system.

## 9. Writing the Conclusion

- Open conclusion.tex.
- Summarize the key points of your project and discuss the outcomes.
- Suggest potential areas for future work and improvements.

## 10. Adding Appendices

- Open appendices.tex.
- Include any additional material that supports your report, such as source code links, or supplementary diagrams.
- Ensure the GitHub link or any other links are accurate.

## 11. Managing References

- Open references.bib.
- Add your bibliography entries in BibTeX format. Ensure all cited works are included here.

## 12. Including Figures

- Place all your images (e.g., block diagrams, system overview diagrams, Gantt charts) in the figures folder.
- Update the \includegraphics commands in the respective .tex files to match the filenames of your images.

## 13. Compiling the Document

- Use Overleaf's "Recompile" button to generate the PDF of your report.
- Review the PDF for formatting issues and ensure all sections are correctly included.

## 14. Final Checks

- Proofread your document for grammatical errors and consistency.
- Ensure all sections are coherent and flow logically.
- Verify that all figures and tables are correctly referenced and labeled.

## Report Grading Rubrics (20pts)
1.  Content & Section Completeness (14)
2. Documentation & Writing Quality (Clarity, Figures, Captions, Screenshots, Citations, Formatting) (6)
